The Steve Jobs Revision

Same content. Restructured as theater, not slides.

ACT I — THE WORLD IS BROKEN
1
The Hook — Scroll-Morph Animation
Identical to current plan. Cars scatter, form circle, morph to arc. Dark canvas, blue radiant glow. Center text: "The operations brain for modern car rental."
You say nothing. Let them watch. The animation does the talking.
2
The Story — "It's Tuesday Afternoon in Amman"
Not bullet points. A narrative scene that unfolds line by line as you scroll:

"It's Tuesday afternoon. Your Airport branch just turned away 3 customers."
↓ scroll "Meanwhile, 4 cars sit idle in Aqaba. Nobody knows."
↓ scroll "The walk-in at the counter has been waiting 20 minutes for a paper contract."
↓ scroll "Finance won't know about any of this until next week."
↓ scroll "This is how most rental operations run today."
Each line appears alone on a dark screen. Massive type. You narrate along. The room goes quiet because they recognize their own operation.
3
The Damage — One Number, Full Screen
Not three numbers competing for attention. One number per scroll-stop. Each fills the entire viewport:

20+ minutes per checkout
That's 20 minutes your customer is standing there questioning their choice.

15% of your fleet is invisible
Cars sitting idle at the wrong branch. Revenue evaporating daily.

Weeks before finance sees reality
By the time the P&L lands, the decision window has closed.
Each number counts up from 0. You pause after each one. Let it sink in. "And it gets worse..." then scroll.
ACT II — THERE IS A BETTER WAY
4
The Reveal — The Logo Moment
Full dark. Silence. Then "Fleetora" scales up from center with a radiant blue-violet glow bloom. Not a tagline dump — just the name, surrounded by light. Like the first time Apple showed the iPhone silhouette.
You say: "We built something." Pause. Scroll. Let the name arrive.
5
Three Revelations — Not "Pillars". Moments.
Each commitment gets a full-screen takeover with its own visual identity:

Screen 5a: "The system thinks with you."
Fleet Brain surfaces what needs attention before you ask. Animated: a subtle pulse visualization showing insights radiating outward from a center node.

Screen 5b: "90 seconds. Walk-in to wheels out."
A countdown timer animation: 90 seconds ticking. Below it, the steps collapsing: ID scan, contract, payment, keys. All automated.

Screen 5c: "Built for Tuesday afternoon."
A dense, beautiful booking detail view fading in — swaps, tickets, multi-driver, mileage tracking. The message: we handle the messy reality, not just the demo.
You say "But wait..." before each scroll. Classic Jobs. Each one is a mini-reveal that builds on the last.
6
The Product — Live, Not Screenshots
Instead of flat screenshots, we build stylized interactive mockups that animate as you scroll. The HQ Cockpit dashboard assembles itself: cards slide in, charts draw, numbers count up, the map populates with vehicle dots. It's not a screenshot — it's the product coming alive.

Three screens, one per role:
• Franchise Head sees the HQ Cockpit assembling
• Branch Staff sees a walk-in checkout completing in real-time
• Finance Admin sees invoices, reconciliation, P&L materializing
You say: "Let me show you what [role] sees every morning." This replaces both the old Screen 6 and 7.
ACT III — THE FUTURE
7
The Compound Effect
A single animated visualization: a line that curves exponentially upward. Below it, three lines appearing in sequence:

"Every booking becomes training data."
"Every return feeds back into pricing."
"Every decision sharpens the next one."

Then, below: "Your competitors can't copy an operational advantage that compounds daily."
This is the "why now" moment. Not features — trajectory.
8
The Close — "We Think This Changes Everything"
Dark. Quiet. The Fleetora mark, small and centered. Below it, one line:

"Built in Jordan. For the operators who refuse to run blind."

Then your name, title, and a single email. Nothing else. No "Book a demo" button. No social links. Confidence.
You close your laptop lid. "Questions?"
What Changed From Version 1
• Problem section is now a story, not bullet points
• Numbers shown one at a time, full screen — not three competing
• "Three pillars" became three reveals with "but wait..." pacing
• Static screenshots replaced with animated product build-ups
• Role table merged into product screens — show, don't tell
• Close is conviction, not a CTA button
• Whole deck restructured as three acts with emotional arc
