Pitch Deck Structure

How should the sales narrative flow? Two proven approaches for $10M+ enterprise pitches.

A

The Apple Keynote Flow

Full-screen sections, one idea per screen, scroll-driven reveals.

Each section is a full viewport height. Scroll triggers transitions between them. Text fades in as you arrive. Numbers count up. Product screenshots scale into view. The presenter controls pacing by scrolling.

Structure (8 screens):
1. Hero animation (scroll-morph) → 2. "Your fleet is flying blind" (problem) → 3. Three pain stats with count-up → 4. "Meet Fleetora" (reveal) → 5. Three commitments (Intelligence / Speed / Depth) → 6. Product showcase (cockpit, POS, booking) → 7. Who it's for (3 roles) → 8. The close

Recommended. This is exactly how Apple presents. One message lands before the next appears. Maximum dramatic impact. The scroll-morph hero flows naturally into this.

B

The Narrative Scroll

Continuous scroll with parallax sections that blend into each other.

More like a Stripe or Vercel marketing page. Sections flow into each other with parallax effects, sticky elements, and progressive reveals. Less "presentation", more "story". The client can also explore it on their own after the meeting.

Structure:
Same content as A, but sections blend rather than snap. Sticky headers, floating product mockups, gradient transitions between themes. More fluid, less theatrical.

Good for leave-behind. Less presenter control — harder to time dramatic pauses.
