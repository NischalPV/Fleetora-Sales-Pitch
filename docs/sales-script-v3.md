**[Start with the most visceral feature — the thing that makes people go "I want that"]**

> "Everything in Fleetora starts here. ⌘K."

> "From here, your branch agent can create a walk-in booking in 90 seconds: scan ID, auto-fill customer profile, check corporate credit limits in real time, generate the contract, process payment. Walk-in to wheels out."

Below: the 90-second walk-in flow as a horizontal step sequence (ID → Profile → Credit → Contract → Payment → Keys), each step with a small icon.

## Slide 4: Booking Detail — The Depth
**[This is where you show that Fleetora isn't a toy. It handles the messy reality.]**

> "Let me open one of these bookings and show you what's underneath."

> "This is the booking detail view — the deepest screen in the product."

> "**Live GPS tracking.** When a vehicle is checked out, you see it on the map. Speed, heading, fuel level, position — updating continuously. If a car enters a geo-fence you set, you get an alert."

> "**Multi-driver management.** This booking has three drivers — the primary renter plus two additional. Each with their own license status, age-based surcharges, and insurance tier. One driver is under 25, so a young-driver surcharge was applied automatically."

> "**Mileage tracking.** Daily allowance of 250km. The system tracks actual usage per day. If they go over, the excess charge calculates automatically — no manual reconciliation."

> "**Vehicle swaps.** The customer's original car had an AC issue on day 3. Your agent swapped them into a Sonata, logged the reason, got manager authorization. The full history is here — date, reason, who approved it."

> "**Traffic tickets.** A speed camera ticket came in for this rental. It's linked to the booking, to the specific driver, with paid/pending/disputed status. When the customer returns, this is part of the checkout settlement."

> "**Payment breakdown.** Base rental, surcharges, extra mileage, insurance, tickets — every line item rolled up into one view. The customer signs off on the total at return."

> "This is what 'depth' means. Every edge case that happens in real operations — swaps, fines, multi-driver families, corporate overrides — is a first-class feature, not an afterthought."

**Visual:** The booking detail screen — THE most impressive mockup in the deck. Split into panels: GPS map with vehicle pin on left, timeline in the middle (with done/current/upcoming states), driver cards on the right with badges (Primary, Under-25, Senior). Below: mileage bar chart, swap history table, ticket ledger, payment breakdown. This screen should make an operator think "finally, someone built software for how we actually work."
**Duration:** 4-5 minutes (this is the centerpiece — spend time here)

## Slide 6: Fleet Brain — The Intelligence Layer
**[The differentiator. This is why they buy Fleetora, not the competition.]**

> "Everything I've shown you so far is what your team uses. Now let me show you what works behind the scenes."

> "Fleet Brain is the intelligence engine built into Fleetora. It monitors every branch, every vehicle, every booking — and it does three things no dashboard can do."

> "**Rebalancing recommendations.** Fleet Brain sees that Airport will need 3 more SUVs by Thursday based on incoming bookings. It sees that Beach has 4 idle SUVs. It generates a transfer recommendation with a confidence score. You accept, override, or dismiss — and it learns from your choice."

> "**Proactive maintenance scheduling.** The system tracks service intervals for every vehicle. When a car is approaching its next service, Fleet Brain finds a low-demand window — a day when that branch has excess inventory — and schedules the service there. No surprise breakdowns. No revenue-generating cars sitting in workshops during peak."

> "**Demand prediction.** Historical patterns plus real-time signals. Fleet Brain knows that the last 3 years, your Airport branch saw a 40% demand spike in the first week of July. It starts recommending fleet positioning in late June."

> "And here's the key: **Fleet Brain gets smarter the longer you use it.** After 3 months, it stops giving generic recommendations. It gives YOUR recommendations — trained on your fleet, your market, your decisions. After 6 months, you have an operations brain that no competitor can replicate because it's built on your data."

**Visual:** The constellation diagram — large and data-rich. But anchored by a SPECIFIC recommendation card in the foreground: "Recommendation: Move 2 SUVs from Beach → Airport | Based on: 3 incoming bookings, 0 SUV availability | Confidence: 94% | Historical accuracy: 89% | [Accept] [Override]". Show data pulses flowing between branch nodes. Below: a small "learning curve" graph showing Fleet Brain accuracy improving over time (60% month 1 → 89% month 6).
**Duration:** 3-4 minutes

## Slide 7: Transfers & Maintenance
**[Show the operational depth — these are the workflows that differentiate]**

> "Let me show you two workflows that most fleet tools completely ignore."

> "**Inter-branch transfers.** Today, transfers happen over WhatsApp or phone calls. With Fleetora, a transfer is a tracked order. You create it, assign vehicles, set a deadline. The branch confirms dispatch, the system tracks the vehicle in transit via GPS, and the receiving branch confirms arrival. Full accountability, full trail."

> "**Maintenance kanban.** Your service scheduling lives on a board — like Trello, but built for fleet ops. Drag a vehicle from 'Due' to 'In Service' to 'Ready.' The board is tied to the booking calendar, so you never schedule service during peak demand. And when a vehicle is in service, it's automatically excluded from availability — no customer gets assigned a car that's in the workshop."

> "These aren't features we added because they look good on a checklist. These are the workflows that eat 2 hours of your ops manager's day. We turned them into 2 minutes."

**Visual:** Split screen. Left: Transfer order view — vehicle list, origin/destination branches, GPS tracking on a mini-map, status timeline (Created → Dispatched → In Transit → Arrived). Right: Maintenance kanban board — three columns (Due, In Service, Ready) with vehicle cards showing model, branch, and service type. Both should look like real product screens.
**Duration:** 2-3 minutes

## Slide 8: Corporate Accounts
**[For franchise operators, corporate accounts are 40-60% of revenue. This matters.]**

> "Let me talk about corporate accounts — because for most franchise operators, this is where the real revenue lives."

> "With Fleetora, every corporate account has a live dashboard. Credit limit, current utilization, billing cycle, authorized employees, rate agreements."

> "When a branch agent creates a booking under a corporate account, the system checks the credit limit in real time. Not against a spreadsheet that's three days stale — against the live balance. If the account is approaching its limit, the system flags it. If it's over, the booking is blocked until approval."

> "Invoicing is automatic. At the end of each billing cycle, Fleetora generates the invoice based on actual usage — every rental, every surcharge, every extra day. No manual compilation. No 'we forgot to include the late return from the 15th.'"

> "And the account manager at HQ can see, at any time: which corporate accounts are growing, which are declining, which are overdue on payment, and which are approaching renewal."

**Visual:** Corporate account dashboard mockup. Show: company name, credit limit bar (used vs. remaining), list of active rentals under the account, billing history, employee roster with rental permissions. Below: an auto-generated invoice preview showing line items. This should make someone who manages corporate accounts think "this would save me a full day every week."
**Duration:** 2-3 minutes
